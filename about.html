STORYTELLER, CITY LOVER, &
TROUBLEMAKER.
Let's learn from each other.
I am constantly soaking up the world and imagining the “what could be’s”. Sinking my toes in the sand, I wonder how I could bottle up the crashing waves and extract their serenity. I feel inspired by spontaneous life conversations at my favorite cafe, Asha Tea House, in Berkeley. Once, someone next to me was editing photos with such beautiful colors and I couldn’t help but ask where he took them and what his editing process was. Talking to people from different walks of life motivates me to see the world from various perspectives and question the status quo, especially in design. I believe design - what it is, how it is carried out - is never finished, but rather continuously evolving.
Aside from being a growing designer, I’m also a senior student at UC Berkeley majoring in Cognitive Science, a cafe enthusiast, a dance team director and choreographer, a writer, and maker of things. Although, I have been dancing for only three years, it became a large part of my life. Dance has taught me a lot about perseverance, confidence, leading by example, and has helped me tremendously in design. To me, both dance and design are forms of story-telling. Creating choreography for a song, arranging formations on stage, and crafting a full set performance develops my creativity. It also helps me to engage human emotions in a very raw form. Such a perspective enables me to find innovative solutions through design.
